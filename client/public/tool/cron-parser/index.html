Frequently Asked Questions

What is a cron expression?

A cron expression defines a schedule using 5 fields: minute (0-59), hour (0-23), day of month (1-31), month (1-12), and day of week (0-6). For example, '0 9 * * 1' means every Monday at 9:00 AM.

What do the special characters mean?

* means any value, , separates multiple values, - defines ranges, / defines step values. For example, */5 in the minute field means every 5 minutes.

What is the difference between 5-field and 6-field cron?

Standard cron uses 5 fields (minute through day-of-week). Some systems add a 6th field for seconds at the beginning.
